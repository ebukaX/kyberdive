# Kyberdive – Sector Definitions (MVP)

## Purpose of This Document

This document defines the **thematic sectors** used by Kyberdive.

Sectors represent **real-world domains of activity** whose forces influence crypto markets.  
They are not categories of tokens — they are **economic and technological narratives**.

This document is the source of truth for:
- Sector naming
- Sector scope
- Narrative boundaries
- Asset inclusion rules

---

## Core Rule

> A sector exists only if it represents a **real-world force** that can independently shape crypto market behavior.

---

## MVP Sectors

---

## 1. Energy

### Definition
The Energy sector represents crypto narratives tied to energy production, distribution, efficiency, and consumption.

### Real-World Drivers
- Power grid modernization
- Renewable energy adoption
- Energy costs and scarcity
- Bitcoin mining energy dynamics
- Infrastructure investment

### Crypto Relevance
- Mining-related infrastructure
- Energy efficiency protocols
- Decentralized energy markets

### Inclusion Criteria
- Direct link to energy production or optimization
- Infrastructure-level relevance

### Exclusions
- General “green” branding without energy mechanics
- Meme or speculative tokens

---

## 2. Artificial Intelligence (AI / Cloud)

### Definition
Represents narratives around automation, machine intelligence, compute infrastructure, and data processing.

### Real-World Drivers
- AI regulation and policy
- Cloud compute demand
- Enterprise automation
- Data center expansion

### Crypto Relevance
- Decentralized compute
- AI data marketplaces
- Infrastructure protocols

### Inclusion Criteria
- Infrastructure or tooling for AI systems
- Data or compute enablement

---

## 3. Quantum Computing

### Definition
Early-stage narratives tied to quantum research, security implications, and future computational shifts.

### Real-World Drivers
- Research breakthroughs
- Government funding
- Cryptography disruption discussions

### Crypto Relevance
- Post-quantum security narratives
- Long-horizon infrastructure concerns

### Inclusion Criteria
- Explicit connection to quantum research or security

### Notes
This is a **low-volume, high-speculation** sector by design.

---

## 4. Semiconductors / Chips

### Definition
Represents hardware supply chains enabling computation, AI, mining, and network infrastructure.

### Real-World Drivers
- Chip shortages
- Geopolitical supply chain risk
- Hardware acceleration demand

### Crypto Relevance
- Mining hardware
- Network performance scaling
- AI infrastructure dependency

---

## 5. Finance (DeFi & TradFi Bridge)

### Definition
Covers financial primitives, payment systems, and bridges between traditional and decentralized finance.

### Real-World Drivers
- Interest rates
- Financial regulation
- Institutional adoption

### Crypto Relevance
- DeFi protocols
- Tokenized assets
- Settlement infrastructure

### Exclusions
- Pure speculation without financial function

Base-layer systems that enable networks to function at scale.

### Real-World Drivers

- Data throughput demand
- Network reliability needs

### Crypto Relevance
- Layer 1s
- Scaling solutions
- Storage networks

---

## 7. Gaming

Interactive digital economies driven by play, ownership, and virtual worlds.

### Real-World Drivers
- Gaming industry growth
- Digital ownership norms
- Virtual economies

### Crypto Relevance
- Game economies
- Asset ownership mechanics

---

## 8. Data

### Definition
The creation, storage, access, and monetization of data.

### Real-World Drivers
- Data privacy regulation
- AI data hunger
- Enterprise data management

### Crypto Relevance
- Decentralized storage
- Data marketplaces
- Privacy-preserving systems

---

## Sector Boundaries (Very Important)

- A token may belong to **multiple narratives**, but Kyberdive assigns **primary sector relevance only** in MVP.
- Tokens are included as **evidence**, not drivers.
- Sectors evolve, but definitions change slowly.

---

## Governance Rule (Future)

Sector definitions may evolve based on:
- Structural shifts in technology
- Policy changes
- Market maturity

All changes must be documented and versioned.

---

## Final Principle

> If a crypto asset cannot be explained through a real-world force, it does not belong in Kyberdive.
